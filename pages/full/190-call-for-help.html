Where do we go from here?

Malware in Python is a thing: it isn’t going to abate. This list is from github projects listed under the tag "python-malware".

If people are interested in being able to understand Python malware in order to thwart it, more people are going to have to step up to the plate to either work on code, or support and fund others to work on it.

I’m not saying that you have to pay me, or that the projects I have described here have to be worked on. While the other decompilers for Python are generally not as good, more to the point, their activity and responsiveness to bug is far below than what I can do for the decompilers I work on. Not surprisingly, those projects are no better funded than the ones I work on.

AI with the right amount of work may help. But that too is going to require a lot more work. The results I have seen so far aren’t very good. Just as with these decompilers, every year, the learning model will need to be updated and the training adjusted, due to bytecode and the language change. Language drift here is much greater than human language drift. [NEXT]
